Feeling worried, stressful during the pandemic? You are not alone.

According to World Health Organization, Fear, worry, and stress are normal responses to perceived or real threats, and at times when we are faced with uncertainty or the unknown. So it is normal and understandable that people are experiencing fear in the context of the COVID-19 pandemic.

Before & during COVID-19 in the U.S.

Average share of adults reporting symptoms of anxiety disorder and/or depressive disorder

January - June 2019: 1.1%

Data Source: National Health Interview Survey

Average share of adults reporting symptoms of anxiety disorder and/or depressive disorder

May 2020 - January 2021: 3.8%

Data Source: Household Pulse Survey

According to KFF's July 2020 health tracking poll, from March 2020 to July 2020, American adults increasingly report that worry and stress related to the pandemic is taking a negative toll on their mental health.

In addition to reporting that the pandemic has negatively affected their mental wellbeing, About half of the adults that participated in the survey report that worry and stress related to COVID-19 is having a negative impact on their mental health in specific aspects, according to KFF's July 2020 health tracking poll.
